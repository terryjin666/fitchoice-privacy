## 2. What the App does

FitChoice is a passive-analysis subscription advisor for endurance athletes who use Strava. After you connect your Strava account, the App periodically analyzes your recent training activity on a server we operate, and produces a weekly recommendation about whether your current Strava subscription tier (Free or Premium) appears to match your usage. The App does not coach you, prescribe workouts, or modify any of your Strava data.

## 3. Information we collect

### 3.1 Information from Strava

When you connect your Strava account through Strava's official OAuth authorization flow, you grant us read-only access to the following Strava scopes:

- `activity:read_all` — your activities, including private ones
- `profile:read_all` — your Strava profile information

Using these scopes, we receive from Strava on your behalf:

- Your Strava athlete ID and public profile fields (display name, profile picture URL, country, city if you have set it publicly)
- Your activity records (date, type, duration, distance, elevation, average heart rate if available, and similar fields surfaced through the Strava API)
- Your current Strava subscription status (Free or Premium), as exposed by Strava's API

We do **not** receive your Strava email address or password. Strava handles authentication; we never see your Strava credentials.

We do **not** write to your Strava account. We do not post activities, kudos, comments, or any other content to Strava on your behalf.

### 3.2 Information you provide directly

During onboarding, you may provide the following directly within the App:

- For Strava Premium users: your subscription plan type, renewal date, and amount paid (used to compute value-for-money metrics in your weekly recommendation)
- For Strava Free users: your decision intent (whether you are actively considering an upgrade, or comfortable on Free)
- Your notification preferences

This information is sent to and stored on our backend, associated with your account.

### 3.3 Information collected automatically

When you use the App, our backend logs technical request metadata necessary to operate the service:

- Your account's internal user ID (an opaque UUID we issue, unrelated to your Strava ID externally)
- Approximate request timestamps and HTTP status codes
- Aggregate API usage statistics

We do **not** collect IP addresses for tracking purposes, advertising identifiers, device fingerprints, or precise location data.

### 3.4 Crash and stability reporting (Sentry)

To diagnose application crashes and stability issues, the App uses Sentry, a third-party error monitoring service operated by Functional Software, Inc. (sentry.io). When the App crashes or encounters a handled error, Sentry receives:

- A stack trace (programmatic call sequence at the time of the crash)
- iOS version, device model, and app version
- An anonymous installation ID generated by Sentry

Our Sentry configuration is deliberately minimized for privacy:

- Performance tracing is disabled (`tracesSampleRate = 0.0`)
- Profiling is disabled
- Screenshots and view hierarchies are **not** captured
- Personally identifiable information is **not** intentionally attached to crash reports

You can request that we exclude your installation from crash reporting at any time by emailing the address in Section 1.

Sentry's own privacy policy is available at https://sentry.io/privacy/.

### 3.5 HealthKit (future versions)

A future version of the App may request your permission to read a limited window of your HealthKit workout history (approximately the last 90 days) and your calendar events for the purpose of refining cold-start recommendations and surfacing decision support before scheduled training. If and when this feature is enabled, the following will hold:

- HealthKit and Calendar data will be processed **on your device only** and will **not** be transmitted to our backend in raw form
- You will be asked for permission through the standard iOS prompt and may revoke it at any time in the iOS Settings app
- Aggregated, non-identifying summary statistics derived from this data may be sent to our backend solely to personalize your weekly recommendation

The current version of the App does **not** read HealthKit or Calendar data.

## 4. How we use your information

We use the information described above only for the following purposes:

1. **Operating the recommendation service**: computing your weekly subscription-fit score and producing the textual recommendation surfaced in the App
2. **Authenticating your account**: maintaining the link between your Strava account and your in-App account across sessions
3. **Operational reliability**: diagnosing crashes, maintaining service availability, and detecting abuse of our backend
4. **Communicating with you**: sending the in-App push notifications and (where applicable) email notifications you have opted into

We do **not** use your data for advertising, retargeting, or behavioral profiling.

We do **not** sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party.

We do **not** use your Strava data, or any data derived from it, to train, fine-tune, or evaluate machine-learning models or artificial intelligence systems. This restriction is required by Strava's API Agreement (effective 2024-11-11) and we adhere to it without exception.

We display your Strava data only to you, the authenticated user from whom it was obtained. We do not display, aggregate, or expose your Strava data to other users of the App. This restriction is also required by Strava's API Agreement.

## 5. Where your data is stored

- **Strava OAuth tokens** (`access_token`, `refresh_token`) are stored on our backend infrastructure (currently hosted on Vercel and Supabase, both US-based). Tokens are encrypted at rest. The iOS App itself never sees or stores these tokens.
- **Your account UUID** is stored in the iOS Keychain on your device. This is the only credential the App holds locally.
- **Cached recommendation results and report data** are stored on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework, for offline display. This cache is cleared when you sign out.
- **Non-sensitive display data** (your Strava username and athlete ID, both publicly visible on Strava itself) are stored in iOS UserDefaults for fast launch.

Backend data is hosted in the United States. If you use the App from outside the United States, your data will be transferred to and processed in the United States.

## 6. How long we keep your data

- Active accounts: while you continue to use the App
- Sign-out: signing out within the App clears local data on your device but retains your account on our backend, so that signing back in restores your history
- Account deletion: deleting your account through the in-App "Delete Account" option permanently removes your records from our backend within 30 days. Deletion is irreversible.
- Crash reports: Sentry retains crash data for 90 days by default
